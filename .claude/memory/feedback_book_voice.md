The Patchouli welcome book (Phase 6 Quests work, lives at `config/patchouli_books/cozycreate_guide/`) is being written for the user's closest people — friends and family joining the server. The user explicitly rejected formal/impersonal voice ("Welcome, engineer." style).

**Why:** These players are the user's close friends, not a generic audience. He wants them to feel that personally when they open the book.

**How to apply:** Write the book in a warm, host-welcoming-guests voice. First person from the user is fine. Avoid jargon-forward openings, "field guide" framing, or distancing language. Still tight and skimmable (audience is veteran MC players), but the *frame* is "I made this for you," not "here is documentation."
